Land Title Transfer Process in Kenya (2026): Step-by-Step

After you verify land and agree on the price, the next step is transferring ownership legally. This guide explains the full title transfer process in Kenya—documents, consents, stamp duty, timelines, and common delays.

Written by Fridah Kawira
9 min read
Updated: February 3, 2026
Key Takeaway

A proper land title transfer is not just “signing papers.” It’s a legal chain: agreement → clearances → consents → valuation → stamp duty → registration. Skipping steps can invalidate the transfer or expose you to fraud.

In Kenya, land ownership changes only when the transfer is registered at the lands registry. Even if you’ve paid in full, you’re not fully protected until registration is complete.

The Land Title Transfer Process (Step-by-Step)

1

Due Diligence & Agreement

Confirm ownership through an official search, verify beacons, confirm rates/rent clearance, and sign a proper sale agreement. Your advocate should ensure the deposit is protected and the completion timeline is clear.

2

Obtain Land Rates / Rent Clearance

Rates clearance comes from the county government; rent clearance applies for leasehold land. Any arrears should be cleared before transfer.

3

Consent to Transfer

Some land requires consent (e.g., Land Control Board for agricultural land in certain areas). Your advocate will advise what applies to your specific land category.

4

Valuation for Stamp Duty

The government valuer assesses the property value to determine stamp duty. This valuation supports the stamp duty calculation and registration requirements.

5

Pay Stamp Duty

Stamp duty is paid before registration. Your advocate usually handles the assessment and payment proof. Keep receipts and confirmation safely.

6

Sign Transfer Documents

Seller signs the transfer instrument; buyer signs where required. Your advocate ensures IDs, PINs, photos, and supporting documents are properly attached.

7

Lodgement & Registration at Lands Office

Documents are lodged, reviewed, and registered. Once approved, the registry updates ownership records and issues updated ownership documents.

Common Reasons Transfers Delay

Missing clearances • wrong parcel details • mismatched names/IDs • incomplete consent requirements • unpaid arrears • incomplete valuation steps • documents signed incorrectly or without witnesses.

Documents You’ll Commonly Need

  • Original title deed / lease
  • Official search (recent)
  • Land rates clearance (county)
  • Land rent clearance (if leasehold)
  • Seller & buyer IDs + KRA PIN certificates
  • Duly executed transfer instrument
  • Stamp duty payment proof
  • Consent documents (where required)

How Long Does a Title Transfer Take?

Timelines vary by land category, document readiness, and lands office workflows. A clean transaction can move quickly, but delays happen if clearances or consents are missing.

Pro Tip from Fridah

The fastest transfers happen when the seller clears rates/rent early and your advocate prepares all completion documents before the completion date. Most delays are preventable.

FAQs

Is a sale agreement enough to prove I own the land?
No. The sale agreement is important, but ownership is confirmed when the transfer is registered at the lands registry.
Can I transfer land without an advocate?
It’s technically possible, but highly risky. A real estate advocate protects you from document errors, fraud, and missed legal steps.
When should I pay the balance?
Typically on completion, after completion documents are ready. Your advocate structures payments based on safety and the agreement.
